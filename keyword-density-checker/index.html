What is Keyword Density?

Keyword density refers to the percentage of times a keyword or phrase appears on a web page compared to the total number of words on the page. In the context of SEO, keyword density can be used to determine whether a web page is relevant to a specific keyword or keyword phrase.

How to Optimize Density

While there is no "perfect" percentage, most SEO experts suggest keeping your primary keyword density around 1-2%. This ensures that search engines understand your topic without flagging your content as "keyword stuffing."

FAQs

What is keyword stuffing?

Keyword stuffing is the practice of loading a webpage with keywords in an attempt to manipulate a site's ranking in search results. This can lead to penalties from Google.

Does this tool handle stop words?

Yes, our tool filters out common "stop words" like 'the', 'is', and 'at' to give you more meaningful results.
