## What is a [UTM](https://smartgentools.com/utm-builder/) Link Builder?

Before diving into the tool, let's break down the terminology. **[UTM](https://smartgentools.com/utm-builder/)** stands for **Urchin Tracking Module**. Despite the complex-sounding name, [UTM](https://smartgentools.com/utm-builder/) parameters are simply short text tags added to the end of a URL to track the effectiveness of online marketing campaigns across various traffic sources and publishing media.

When a user clicks a URL with [UTM](https://smartgentools.com/utm-builder/) parameters attached, those tags are sent back to your Google Analytics (or other web analytics platform). This data allows you to see exactly where that user came from, how they found you, and what specific piece of content convinced them to click.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

To help you get the most out of your campaign tracking, we have compiled answers to some of the most common questions about [UTM](https://smartgentools.com/utm-builder/) parameters.

### What is [UTM](https://smartgentools.com/utm-builder/) Source?
The [UTM](https://smartgentools.com/utm-builder/) Source (`utm_source`) is a required parameter that tells Google Analytics exactly *who* or *what* is sending the traffic to your site. Think of it as the referrer. Common examples of a [UTM](https://smartgentools.com/utm-builder/) source include `google`, `facebook`, `mailchimp`, `twitter`, or the name of a specific affiliate partner. 

### Are [UTM](https://smartgentools.com/utm-builder/) links case-sensitive?
**Yes, absolutely.** Google Analytics is case-sensitive when it reads [UTM](https://smartgentools.com/utm-builder/) parameters. This means that if you use `utm_source=Facebook` for one link and `utm_source=facebook` for another, Google Analytics will treat them as two completely separate traffic sources. To keep your data clean and easy to read, it is highly recommended to use **all lowercase letters** for every parameter.

### Can I use spaces in [UTM](https://smartgentools.com/utm-builder/) tags?
**It's best to use underscores (`_`) or hyphens (`-`) instead of spaces to avoid encoding issues.** If you use a space in a [UTM](https://smartgentools.com/utm-builder/) parameter (like `summer sale`), browsers will automatically convert that space into `%20` in the URL (making it `summer%20sale`). This looks messy and can sometimes cause tracking errors. Stick to hyphens or underscores (e.g., `summer_sale` or `summer-sale`) for the best results.
